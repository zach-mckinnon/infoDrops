Resisting the Digital Drift

A lightweight guide for the average citizen.

1. Understanding the Warning

This is a new form of control that fuses corporate technology with authoritarian politics to monitor, manipulate, and suppress the public.

What is Fascism?

A system where a small group uses fear and control to stay in power. They silence opponents, use threats, and convince people to give up freedom for "safety."

What makes it "Techno"?

Instead of jackboots, they use your phone. Instead of propaganda posters, they use targeted ads. Your data tells them everything about you, and algorithms control what you see. Big Tech + Government = New Kind of Control.

Why is it a Threat Now?

They know everything: where you go, who you talk to, what you buy, what makes you angry. AI can predict your behavior better than you can. Perfect storm: maximum surveillance + social division = total control.

2. The Citizen's Toolkit

Taking action is the next step. Here are concrete steps any citizen can take.

Defend Your Digital Privacy

Why it Matters:

Limiting data collection makes it harder for them to track, manipulate, or profile you.

How to do it:

  • Use encrypted messaging apps like Signal.
  • Switch to a privacy-focused browser like Brave or Firefox.
  • Use a privacy-respecting search engine like DuckDuckGo.
Engage in Local Politics

Why it Matters:

Local officials (school boards, city councils) have a huge impact on your daily life. National decay often starts with local apathy.

How to do it:

  • Vote in EVERY election, especially local ones.
  • Attend a town hall or city council meeting, even online.
  • Learn who your local representatives are.
Support Independent Media

Why it Matters:

A free press is essential for holding power accountable. Supporting them ensures real journalism can continue.

How to do it:

  • Subscribe to your local newspaper.
  • Donate to non-profit news organizations.
  • Share their work and give them credit.

3. The Foundation: Community

Authoritarianism thrives when we are divided and isolated. The most powerful act of resistance is building community.

Every time you share food, offer help, or organize a local cleanup, you perform a revolutionary act. You replace fear with solidarity. This is the ground from which all other resistance grows.

Stay informed. Stay engaged. Stay connected.
